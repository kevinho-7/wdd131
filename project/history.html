The Birth of a Giant (1910-1920)

On September 1, 1910, a group of workers from the Bom Retiro neighborhood, inspired by the performance of Corinthian-Casuals F.C., founded Sport Club Corinthians Paulista. The club's first president was Miguel Battaglia, and the initial team was formed by working-class individuals who shared a passion for football.

The club's early years were marked by struggle and determination. Playing in the várzea (amateur) championships, Corinthians quickly gained popularity among the working class of São Paulo.

Rise to Glory (1920-1950)

The 1920s marked Corinthians' first golden era, with the club winning its first São Paulo State Championship in 1914. Throughout the following decades, the team established itself as one of the most successful in São Paulo state.

During this period, legendary players like Neco and Teleco emerged, helping to build the club's winning tradition.

Modern Era Achievements

The modern era has seen Corinthians reach unprecedented heights. The club has won multiple national championships, including:

  • FIFA Club World Cup (2012)
  • Copa Libertadores (2012)
  • Brazilian Championship (1990, 1998, 1999, 2005, 2011, 2015)
  • Copa do Brasil (1995, 2002, 2009)
  • Recopa Sudamericana (2013)

Neo Química Arena Era

In 2014, Corinthians inaugurated its new home, the Neo Química Arena. This modern stadium, built to FIFA standards, represents a new chapter in the club's history. With a capacity for over 47,000 spectators, it is considered one of South America's most modern football venues.
