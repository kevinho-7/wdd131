About Corinthians

Sport Club Corinthians Paulista, founded on September 1, 1910, is one of Brazil's most iconic football clubs. Born from the dreams of five workers in the Bom Retiro neighborhood of São Paulo, the club was inspired by the English team Corinthian-Casuals F.C. and named in their honor.

Known as "Timão" and supported by the passionate "Fiel" (faithful fans), Corinthians has grown to become one of the most successful Brazilian clubs. With multiple national and international titles, including the FIFA Club World Cup, Copa Libertadores, and numerous Brazilian Championships, the club represents more than just football - it's a symbol of popular power and resilience.

The club's home, Neo Química Arena, located in São Paulo's east zone, is a modern stadium that exemplifies Corinthians' evolution while maintaining its traditional values. With over 30 million supporters, Corinthians continues to be "the team of the people," embodying the fighting spirit and passion that have characterized it since its humble beginnings.
